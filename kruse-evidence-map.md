## Evidence Tiers Summary

### Tier 1: Strong PubMed Support (>30 papers)
These Kruse claims are backed by substantial peer-reviewed literature:
- Cold + BAT activation (1,317 papers)
- POMC/MSH + UV pathway (163 papers)
- UV releases nitric oxide from skin (143 papers)
- Cytochrome c oxidase absorbs near-infrared light (65 papers)
- Melatonin is synthesized inside mitochondria (37 papers)
- Gut-brain-serotonin axis (36 papers)

### Tier 2: Moderate Support (5-30 papers)
Real science, active research, but not yet mainstream:
- Quantum coherence in electron transport chains (26 papers)
- Leptin sensitivity follows circadian rhythm (17 papers)
- Proton tunneling in enzyme catalysis (5 papers)

### Tier 3: Weak/Emerging (<5 papers)
Small studies, limited replication, or indirect evidence:
- Grounding/earthing (6 papers, small studies)
- EZ water / fourth phase (4 papers, Pollack lab only)
- Deuterium + ATP synthase (1 paper)

### Tier 4: Frontier (0 PubMed papers)
These are Kruse's original syntheses — connecting established physics to biology in ways no one else has published:
- Biological semiconduction (Szent-Gyorgyi 1941, no modern papers)
- DHA membrane quantum electron effects
- Melanin as a photoelectric converter in biology
- Vitamin D sulfate pathway
- Magnetism + brain health (beyond cryptochrome)

**The pattern:** Kruse takes established physics (quantum tunneling, semiconduction, photoelectric effect) and established biology (mitochondria, DHA, melanin) and connects them in ways that haven't been formally studied. His Tier 1 claims are solid. His Tier 4 claims are original hypotheses that need testing.
