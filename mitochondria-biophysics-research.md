## 5. Hypotheses

### Membrane Potential as Master Biomarker
DeltaPsi integrates everything: electron flow, ROS, calcium, apoptosis. Could predict biological age better than epigenetic clocks. 176 papers.
**Test:** DeltaPsi-based age test on blood PBMCs via JC-1 dye. Correlate with GrimAge.

### Cardiolipin Oxidation Is the Root Cause
Cardiolipin holds ETC supercomplexes together. When it oxidizes, everything falls apart. mtDNA damage, ROS increase, biogenesis failure may all be downstream.
**Test:** Measure CL oxidation in young vs old. Test SS-31/elamipretide on supercomplex assembly.

### Red Light Primes Cold Stress Response
PBM restores Complex IV flow. Cold then demands more throughput. Priming with light before cold = stronger adaptation.
**Test:** Red light 10min then cold plunge vs cold alone. Measure ATP, UCP1, BAT thermal imaging.

### Molecular Hydrogen Is the Ideal Mito Intervention
H2 only scavenges hydroxyl radicals, not signaling ROS. Preserves hormesis while eliminating damage. 1,169 papers.
**Test:** H2 + exercise vs exercise alone. Track NRF2/HO-1 preserved while 8-OHdG reduced.

### Quantum Tunneling Efficiency Declines With Age
Electron tunneling through ETC depends on protein geometry. Oxidative damage misshapes proteins. Tunneling drops. More electrons leak. Self-amplifying.
**Test:** Measure electron transfer rates in young vs old mito via stopped-flow spectroscopy.

### The Daily Stack: Light > Cold > Fast > Move
Morning light (circadian ETC regulation) then cold (BAT + biogenesis) then fasted (AMPK + mitophagy) then exercise (PGC-1a). Each amplifies the next via shared pathways.
**Test:** 12-week protocol. Measure mito copy number, DeltaPsi, NAD+/NADH, VO2max, epigenetic age.
